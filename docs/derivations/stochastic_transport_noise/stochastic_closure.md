# R06 stochastic closure and covariance requirements

**Program:** stochastic transport and finite-size noise  
**Contribution:** R06  
**Controlling issue:** #341  
**Status:** Phase 1A requirements; no production covariance implementation authorized

## 1. Purpose

The deterministic drift-diffusion-Poisson equations specify mean transport. They do not uniquely specify the noise. R06 will construct stochastic forcing from elementary transition or transport events and then verify the resulting terminal spectrum against equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation constraints.

The central rule is:

> The covariance of a net process is determined by the sum of the forward and reverse event rates and by their shared stoichiometry, not by the net mean rate alone.

An arbitrary white source added to force Johnson noise or a Lorentzian shape is prohibited.

## 2. State vector

The minimum deterministic state \(\{n,p,\phi\}\) is not automatically sufficient for stochastic SRH dynamics.

The candidate local dynamic state is

\[
u=\{n,p,N_t^{\mathrm{occ}},\phi\},
\]

where \(N_t^{\mathrm{occ}}\) is the density of traps occupied by an electron. The trap charge contribution to Poisson's equation depends on the donor-like or acceptor-like charge convention and must be stated separately.

Contact interfacial populations and an external-circuit state may be appended if required by detailed balance or by the selected terminal loading.

## 3. Event representation

Consider elementary reaction channel \(r\) with:

- local event rate \(a_r(u)\) in \(\mathrm{m^{-3}s^{-1}}\);
- stoichiometric change vector \(\nu_r\) in carrier or trap counts per event.

For a one-dimensional continuum obtained by averaging over cross-sectional area \(A\), define independent Poisson event fields \(\eta_r(x,t)\) with time-domain covariance

\[
\left\langle
\eta_r(x,t)\eta_s(x',t')
\right\rangle
=
\frac{a_r(u_0)}{A}
\delta_{rs}\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t').
\]

The induced state-source covariance is

\[
Q_{\mathrm{rxn}}(x,x')
=
\frac{1}{A}
\sum_r
\nu_r\nu_r^T a_r(u_0)
\delta(x-x').
\]

This is a time-domain white-noise normalization. Factors of two enter only when converting to a selected one-sided spectral convention.

A spatial discretization over cell volume \(A\Delta x_i\) must reproduce the corresponding chemical-Langevin scaling

\[
Q_i
=
\frac{1}{A\Delta x_i}
\sum_r\nu_r\nu_r^Ta_{r,i}.
\]

The discrete covariance must remain positive semidefinite.

## 4. Direct pair generation and recombination

For a direct generation event,

\[
\nu_g=
\begin{bmatrix}
+1\\
+1
\end{bmatrix},
\]

and for a direct recombination event,

\[
\nu_r=
\begin{bmatrix}
-1\\
-1
\end{bmatrix},
\]

in the \((n,p)\) subspace.

If their mean event rates are \(g\) and \(r\), then

\[
Q_{np}^{\mathrm{pair}}
=
\frac{g+r}{A}
\begin{bmatrix}
1&1\\
1&1
\end{bmatrix}
\delta(x-x').
\]

Therefore:

- electron and hole number sources are perfectly positively correlated for a direct pair event;
- the source amplitude remains nonzero at equilibrium even when \(g-r=0\);
- using \(|G-R|\), \(G-R\), or \(2R_{\mathrm{SRH}}\) as a generic intensity is not valid.

Bonani and Ghione explicitly distinguish fundamental bipolar population sources from approximate equivalent current-density sources and show that minority-carrier and boundary effects can invalidate the approximate representation (DOI: `10.1016/S0038-1101(98)00253-6`).

## 5. Explicit SRH trap channels

Let \(N_t^{\mathrm{occ}}\) denote electron-occupied traps. A minimal SRH event decomposition contains four channels:

| Channel | Event | Stoichiometry \((n,p,N_t^{\mathrm{occ}})\) |
|---|---|---|
| electron capture | \(e+T_{\mathrm{empty}}\rightarrow T_{\mathrm{occ}}\) | \((-1,0,+1)\) |
| electron emission | \(T_{\mathrm{occ}}\rightarrow e+T_{\mathrm{empty}}\) | \((+1,0,-1)\) |
| hole capture | \(h+T_{\mathrm{occ}}\rightarrow T_{\mathrm{empty}}\) | \((0,-1,-1)\) |
| hole emission | \(T_{\mathrm{empty}}\rightarrow h+T_{\mathrm{occ}}\) | \((0,+1,+1)\) |

The corresponding propensities are constructed from capture coefficients, carrier densities, occupied/empty trap densities, and equilibrium emission constants. Their exact forms must be traced to the authoritative SRH kinetics and checked under the selected degeneracy approximation.

The mean equations obtained from these four channels may be reduced to the standard algebraic net SRH rate under a quasi-steady trap approximation. That reduction does not prove that the trap fluctuation mode may also be eliminated.

A controlled stochastic elimination requires separation between the trap relaxation rate and every terminally observable transport or recombination mode. The reduced covariance must include the colored and cross-correlated terms generated by the elimination. Otherwise, retain \(N_t^{\mathrm{occ}}\) explicitly.

The foundational mean-kinetic sources are:

- Shockley and Read, DOI `10.1103/PhysRev.87.835`;
- Hall, DOI `10.1103/PhysRev.87.387`.

The HgCdTe-specific treatment by Iverson and Smith reports additional deep-trap effects on free and bound carrier fluctuations, transport, and sweepout (DOI: `10.1063/1.335666`). Full equation verification is required before selecting the final trap state.

## 6. Transport or diffusion flux fluctuations

A primitive fluctuating-hydrodynamic representation assigns a stochastic particle flux to each mobile carrier. For a nondegenerate, isothermal, locally equilibrated species \(c\),

\[
\Gamma_c
=
\Gamma_{c,0}
+
\delta\Gamma_c^{\mathrm{det}}
+
\zeta_c.
\]

The candidate local time-domain covariance is

\[
\left\langle
\zeta_c(x,t)\zeta_c(x',t')
\right\rangle
=
\frac{2D_c c_0}{A}
\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t').
\]

For conventional current, the corresponding primitive factor is multiplied by \(q^2\).

This expression is **provisional** and limited to an ideal nondegenerate diffusion closure. Before implementation it must be checked against:

1. the generalized thermodynamic factor under Fermi-Dirac statistics;
2. field-dependent mobility or hot-carrier conditions;
3. the selected finite-volume face-flux discretization;
4. contact-interface transport;
5. the possibility that a single-particle or impedance-field derivation already includes the same microscopic contribution.

Do not combine a primitive diffusion-flux source with an approximate equivalent current source derived from the same carrier motion.

Bulashenko, Gomila, Rubi, and Kochelap establish terminal transfer-field methods for distributed drift-diffusion noise in inhomogeneous devices (DOI: `10.1063/1.367023`). Gomila and Reggiani show that diffusion and Poisson correlations materially alter finite-size GR current noise (DOI: `10.1063/1.1526915`).

## 7. Contact injection and extraction fluctuations

A finite contact-recombination velocity is not a complete stochastic boundary.

For carrier \(c\) at contact \(k\), write the mean outward event flux as

\[
\nu_k\Gamma_c
=
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{out}}
-
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{in}}.
\]

The boundary fluctuation is generated by the two primitive channels,

\[
\xi_{c,k}
=
\xi_{c,k}^{\mathrm{out}}
-
\xi_{c,k}^{\mathrm{in}},
\]

with covariance proportional to

\[
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{out}}
+
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{in}}.
\]

At equilibrium,

\[
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{out}}
=
a_{c,k}^{\mathrm{in}},
\]

so the mean boundary flux vanishes while its fluctuation intensity remains finite.

Required contact checks:

1. equilibrium detailed balance;
2. blocking limit, in which both transfer propensities vanish;
3. ideal-reservoir limit, in which carrier electrochemical potential is pinned but reservoir exchange noise remains consistent with the terminal ensemble;
4. left/right orientation through the outward normal;
5. electron-hole and trap cross-correlation if one interfacial event changes multiple populations;
6. no double counting with an explicit contact resistance noise source.

Park's finite-surface-recombination theory confirms spectral sensitivity to contact velocity, but Phase 1A must determine from the full text whether contact fluctuations are represented microscopically or only through deterministic boundary conditions (DOI: `10.1063/5.0111954`).

Smith's HgCdTe blocking-contact theory directly overlaps the responsivity and GR-noise problem (DOI: `10.1063/1.334155`). R06 must reproduce its limiting behavior before claiming a new contact-mode result.

## 8. Optical generation fluctuations

The mean optical generation may be uniform or Beer-Lambert distributed. Its stochastic statistics depend on the incident radiation.

Separate cases are required:

- deterministic modulated source used only for responsivity;
- coherent or shot-noise-limited illumination, approximated by Poisson photon arrivals;
- thermal background with bunching and optical-band correlations;
- uncertain source fluctuations propagated through the measured optical chain.

Optical pair generation has the same \((+1,+1)\) carrier stoichiometry as thermal generation, but its temporal and spatial covariance need not be white or local.

Photon-source noise must not be mislabeled as intrinsic detector GR noise.

## 9. Displacement current

No independent displacement-current Langevin source is added for an electroquasistatic, lossless, constant-\(\varepsilon\) dielectric. Displacement current transduces the charge and field fluctuations generated by carrier, trap, contact, and circuit processes.

If the dielectric is lossy or dispersive, its polarization degrees of freedom and associated fluctuation-dissipation source must be included. Substituting a complex permittivity while omitting its thermal fluctuation source is not equilibrium-consistent.

## 10. External circuit

A finite source or load impedance introduces its own thermal source at its physical temperature.

The device and external circuit must be solved in the same small-signal system when testing:

- open-circuit voltage noise;
- short-circuit current noise;
- loaded transimpedance output;
- finite-bias current or voltage fluctuations.

An ideal voltage source clamps \(\delta V\); an ideal current source clamps \(\delta I\). The complementary terminal observable may still fluctuate.

At equilibrium, the assembled two-terminal model must recover

\[
S_V^{(1)}(f)
=
4k_BT\operatorname{Re}Z(2\pi f)
\]

or

\[
S_I^{(1)}(f)
=
4k_BT\operatorname{Re}Y(2\pi f)
\]

under the one-sided hertz convention.

## 11. No-double-counting rules

The following combinations are prohibited unless their statistical independence is derived:

1. fundamental population GR sources plus an equivalent GR current-density source;
2. primitive diffusion-flux sources plus a single-particle current source describing the same motion;
3. stochastic contact exchange plus an independent lumped contact-resistance source derived from the same interface;
4. dynamic trap occupancy plus an additional Lorentzian trap source;
5. device Johnson noise plus an arbitrary resistor source inserted solely to force the correct total;
6. optical shot noise counted both in photon generation and in an added photocurrent source.

Each source must map to a distinct microscopic event family or independent external subsystem.

## 12. Linearized covariance assembly

After spatial discretization,

\[
M\delta\dot u
=
J\delta u+B\xi,
\]

with primitive covariance

\[
\left\langle
\xi(t)\xi^T(t')
\right\rangle
=
Q_\xi\delta(t-t').
\]

The state covariance at stable equilibrium must satisfy the continuous Lyapunov equation

\[
J C M^T
+
M C J^T
+
B Q_\xi B^T
=
0
\]

after accounting for algebraic constraints or reducing to the dynamic subspace.

This equation provides a zero-frequency-independent check of the same covariance used in the frequency solver. The integrated frequency-domain state PSD must reproduce \(C\).

Required numerical checks:

- \(Q_\xi=Q_\xi^T\);
- \(Q_\xi\) positive semidefinite within roundoff;
- conservation-law null spaces treated explicitly;
- equilibrium state covariance agrees with thermodynamic compressibility where available;
- integrated terminal PSD agrees with time-domain or Lyapunov covariance;
- terminal FDT error reported separately from nonlinear and linear solver residuals.

## 13. Present closure decision

### Retain explicitly in the baseline design

- electron density \(n\);
- hole density \(p\);
- electrostatic potential \(\phi\);
- at least one dynamic trap-occupancy variable when trap-assisted GR is active;
- terminal circuit variable when impedance is finite;
- primitive pair, trap, transport, and contact event channels unless a verified equivalent formulation replaces them.

### Deferred

- radiative and Auger event channels;
- hot-carrier temperature;
- dielectric loss;
- broad trap distributions intended to generate true \(1/f\) noise;
- three-dimensional contact fluctuations.

### Rejected as baseline shortcuts

- algebraic SRH mean rate used directly as a white-noise intensity;
- independent electron and hole sources for one pair event;
- deterministic finite-\(S\) contact without an equilibrium source audit;
- a single imposed Lorentzian covariance;
- empirical lifetime correction used to repair a structurally incomplete source model.

## 14. Required full-text audits before implementation
