# R06 contact Onsager–FDT closure and singular limits

**Program:** stochastic transport and finite-size noise  
**Contribution:** R06  
**Issues:** #341 and #343

## 1. Purpose

This document converts the forward/reverse contact-event model into a linear nonequilibrium-thermodynamic form and derives:

- the equilibrium fluctuation–dissipation relation;
- the finite-volume state/terminal covariance block;
- the blocking-contact limit;
- the fast-reservoir/Dirichlet limit;
- the distinction between independent exchange, pair recombination, and interface-state mediation;
- numerical acceptance tests.

The derivation is intended to remain valid even if the unavailable Park 2022 paper contains overlapping deterministic boundary equations. The general stochastic-interface concept is presumed established by adjacent literature and is not a novelty claim.

## 2. Sign convention

At contact `c`, let the outward normal point from the semiconductor into the reservoir. For carrier species `s`, define the outward particle flux

\[
\Gamma_{s,c}^{out}>0
\]

when particles leave the semiconductor.

Let

\[
\Delta\mu_{s,c}=\mu_s-\mu_{s,c}^{res}
\]

be the semiconductor electrochemical potential relative to the reservoir.

Positive `Delta mu` must drive positive outward particle flux.

## 3. Forward and reverse event rates

Let the primitive event rates per unit contact area be

\[
a^{out}_{s,c},
\qquad
a^{in}_{s,c},
\]

with units `m^-2 s^-1`.

The mean outward flux and event activity are

\[
\overline{\Gamma}_{s,c}^{out}
=a^{out}_{s,c}-a^{in}_{s,c},
\]

\[
\mathcal A_{s,c}
=a^{out}_{s,c}+a^{in}_{s,c}.
\]

The primitive area-averaged white flux source `eta` satisfies

\[
\langle\eta_{s,c}(t)\eta_{s',c'}(t')\rangle
=
\delta_{ss'}\delta_{cc'}
\frac{\mathcal A_{s,c}}{A_c}
\delta(t-t')
\]

when the forward and reverse event streams are independent Poisson processes.

At equilibrium,

\[
a^{out}_{eq}=a^{in}_{eq}=a_{eq},
\]

so

\[
\overline{\Gamma}^{out}_{eq}=0,
\qquad
\mathcal A_{eq}=2a_{eq}.
\]

## 4. Local detailed balance

The event rates must satisfy

\[
\ln\left(\frac{a^{out}}{a^{in}}\right)
=
\frac{\Delta\mu}{k_BT}
\]

for the selected outward-event convention.

This condition determines the ratio of the rates, not their sum. A kinetic model is still required to specify the absolute activity.

### 4.1 Classical finite-velocity closure

For a nondegenerate boundary density `c`, choose

\[
a^{out}=Sc,
\qquad
a^{in}=Sc_{eq}.
\]

Since

\[
\frac{c}{c_{eq}}
=
\exp\left(\frac{\Delta\mu}{k_BT}\right),
\]

local detailed balance is satisfied exactly.

The mean flux is

\[
\overline{\Gamma}^{out}=S(c-c_{eq}),
\]

and the activity is

\[
\mathcal A=S(c+c_{eq}).
\]

At equilibrium,

\[
\mathcal A_{eq}=2Sc_{eq}.
\]

## 5. Linear Onsager form

Near equilibrium,

\[
c-c_{eq}
=
\left(\frac{\partial c}{\partial\mu}\right)_{eq}
\Delta\mu+O(\Delta\mu^2).
\]

Define the equilibrium thermodynamic susceptibility

\[
\chi_c
=
\left(\frac{\partial c}{\partial\mu}\right)_{eq}.
\]

Then

\[
\overline{\Gamma}^{out}
=K_c\Delta\mu+O(\Delta\mu^2),
\qquad
K_c=S\chi_c.
\]

For a classical nondegenerate carrier,

\[
\chi_c=\frac{c_{eq}}{k_BT},
\]

so

\[
K_c=\frac{Sc_{eq}}{k_BT}.
\]

Using the thermodynamic force

\[
X_c=\frac{\Delta\mu}{T},
\]

the Onsager coefficient is

\[
L_c=T K_c.
\]

For the classical closure,

\[
L_c=\frac{Sc_{eq}}{k_B}.
\]

## 6. Interface fluctuation–dissipation relation

Linear fluctuating thermodynamics gives the area-averaged equilibrium flux covariance

\[
\langle\eta_c(t)\eta_c(t')\rangle_{eq}
=
\frac{2k_BL_c}{A_c}
\delta(t-t').
\]

Since `L_c=T K_c`, this is equivalently

\[
\boxed{
\langle\eta_c(t)\eta_c(t')\rangle_{eq}
=
\frac{2k_BT K_c}{A_c}
\delta(t-t')
}
\]

For the classical finite-velocity closure,

\[
2k_BT K_c=2Sc_{eq},
\]

which exactly matches the forward-plus-reverse Poisson activity:

\[
\frac{2Sc_{eq}}{A_c}.
\]

This equivalence is an internal consistency test between event counting and Onsager FDT.

The factor of two is generated by equal equilibrium forward and reverse activity. It is separate from one-sided versus two-sided PSD conversion.

## 7. Degenerate or nonparabolic carrier statistics

The general equilibrium relation is controlled by `chi_c`, not by `c_eq/(k_B T)`.

For a degenerate carrier reservoir,

\[
K_c=S\chi_c
\]

only if `S` is defined as the linear density-relaxation velocity in

\[
\Gamma^{out}=S(c-c_{eq}).
\]

The equilibrium flux covariance is then

\[
Q_{\Gamma,eq}
=
\frac{2k_BT S\chi_c}{A_c}.
\]

Using

\[
Q_{\Gamma,eq}=\frac{2Sc_{eq}}{A_c}
\]

outside the Boltzmann regime would generally be inconsistent unless the microscopic rates independently justify it.

R06 must therefore compute or bound `chi_c` for the selected HgCdTe statistics before quantitative contact-noise claims.

## 8. Boundary finite-volume assembly

Let the boundary control volume have thickness `h` and volume

\[
V_b=A_c h.
\]

A positive outward event removes one particle from the semiconductor cell. The contact contribution to the boundary density equation is

\[
\frac{d\,\delta c_b}{dt}
=
-\frac{S}{h}\delta c_b
-\frac{1}{h}\eta_c(t)
\]

for the linear classical closure, ignoring coupling to the interior for this local derivation.

Define

\[
\lambda_c=\frac{S}{h}.
\]

The density-source covariance is

\[
Q_{cc}
=
\frac{Q_{\Gamma}}{h^2}.
\]

At equilibrium,

\[
Q_{cc,eq}
=
\frac{2Sc_{eq}}{A_c h^2}.
\]

## 9. State-terminal covariance from one primitive event

Let the terminal-current contribution of an outward particle event be

\[
\delta I_c=z_s q A_c\eta_c,
\]

where `z_s` contains the carrier-charge and terminal-orientation sign.

The same primitive source produces both the boundary density source and the direct terminal-current source. Define the source vector

\[
b_c=
\begin{bmatrix}
-1/h\\
z_s qA_c
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

Then the combined state-terminal covariance is

\[
Q_c=Q_{\Gamma}\,b_cb_c^T.
\]

Explicitly,

\[
Q_c
=Q_{\Gamma}
\begin{bmatrix}
1/h^2 & -z_s qA_c/h\\
-z_s qA_c/h & q^2A_c^2
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

This matrix is rank one and positive semidefinite.

The off-diagonal term is not optional. It records that the terminal charge transfer and boundary population change are the same event.

Adding an independent boundary density source and an independent contact current source would replace this rank-one covariance by an incorrect block-diagonal covariance and double count the process.

## 10. Boundary-cell equilibrium variance

For the isolated linear contact relaxation,

\[
\frac{d\delta c_b}{dt}
=-\lambda_c\delta c_b+\zeta_c,
\]

with

\[
\langle\zeta_c(t)\zeta_c(t')\rangle
=
\frac{2Sc_{eq}}{A_ch^2}\delta(t-t'),
\]

the stationary variance is

\[
\langle\delta c_b^2\rangle
=
\frac{Q_{cc}}{2\lambda_c}
=
\frac{c_{eq}}{A_ch}
=
\frac{c_{eq}}{V_b}.
\]

This is the expected classical number-fluctuation result

\[
\operatorname{Var}(N_b)=c_{eq}V_b.
\]

The pointwise density variance diverges as `h -> 0`, as expected for continuum delta-correlated density fluctuations. Mesh convergence must therefore be evaluated on integrated populations and terminal observables, not on pointwise variance.

## 11. Boundary-density PSD

The two-sided angular-frequency density PSD for the isolated boundary mode is

\[
S_{c_b}^{(2)}(\omega)
=
\frac{2Sc_{eq}/(A_ch^2)}
{\omega^2+(S/h)^2}.
\]

Its integral gives `c_eq/V_b`.

The corner frequency is

\[
\omega_c=\frac{S}{h}.
\]

This corner is a discretized boundary-exchange rate, not a material recombination lifetime. In a continuum solution it merges with the boundary-layer spectrum and must not be interpreted as a detector GR lifetime.

## 12. Blocking limit

As

\[
S\rightarrow0,
\]

both the deterministic exchange rate and primitive exchange activity vanish:

\[
\lambda_c\rightarrow0,
\qquad
Q_{\Gamma}\rightarrow0.
\]

Consequences:

1. particle exchange through that boundary vanishes;
2. contact exchange noise vanishes;
3. displacement-current coupling can remain;
4. if no bulk process relaxes the associated conserved population, a zero or near-zero eigenvalue appears;
5. the low-frequency spectrum can become singular or initial-condition dependent if the external ensemble is not specified.

A blocking-contact test must therefore inspect the operator null space rather than merely setting `S=0` and solving the same invertible system.

## 13. Fast-reservoir limit

As

\[
S\rightarrow\infty,
\]

\[
\lambda_c=\frac{S}{h}\rightarrow\infty.
\]

At fixed finite frequency,

\[
S_{c_b}^{(2)}(\omega)
\sim
\frac{2c_{eq}}{A_cS}
\rightarrow0.
\]

Thus the boundary density becomes spectrally pinned over any finite observation band, consistent with a Dirichlet reservoir.

However, the instantaneous variance remains

\[
\frac{c_{eq}}{V_b}
\]

because the fluctuation bandwidth grows without bound. Therefore the Dirichlet limit is singular in time and cannot be obtained by deleting the contact source before eliminating the fast state.

The primitive flux covariance grows as

\[
Q_{\Gamma,eq}=\frac{2Sc_{eq}}{A_c}\rightarrow\infty.
\]

Its contribution to a finite terminal observable is regularized by:

- the vanishing contact impedance;
- the fast boundary response;
- state-terminal cross covariance;
- bulk/contact transfer functions;
- external circuit loading.

Individual source components may diverge while the complete terminal PSD remains finite. The limit must be taken after assembling the full covariance and observation operator.

## 14. Series-resistance sanity check

For an equilibrium bulk resistance `R_b` in series with a contact resistance `R_c`, the short-circuit current noise is

\[
S_I^{(1)}
=
\frac{4k_BT}{R_b+R_c}.
\]

The contact voltage-noise contribution scales as

\[
4k_BTR_c.
\]

As `R_c -> 0`, the contact contribution vanishes and the terminal result approaches the bulk value.

Representing the same contact as a Norton current source gives an intrinsic source proportional to `1/R_c`, which diverges as `R_c -> 0`; its transfer to the series terminal vanishes sufficiently rapidly. This circuit limit illustrates why a divergent primitive contact-flux source is not itself unphysical.

## 15. Independent carrier exchange

If electrons and holes exchange independently with separate reservoirs or independent channels, the primitive contact covariance is block diagonal in the electron and hole event families:

\[
Q_{np}^{contact}=0
\]

before electrostatic and transport propagation.

Electron-hole terminal correlations can still emerge dynamically through Poisson coupling and recombination.

## 16. Pair recombination at an interface

A surface pair-recombination event changes both populations:

\[
\nu_{rec}=(-1,-1),
\]

while the reverse pair-generation event has

\[
\nu_{gen}=(+1,+1).
\]

The primitive covariance is

\[
Q_{np}^{pair}
=(a_{rec}+a_{gen})
\begin{bmatrix}
1&1\\
1&1
\end{bmatrix}
\]

with the proper area/volume normalization.

This is not equivalent to two independent Robin boundaries with the same numerical `S`.

## 17. Dynamic interface-state mediation

If an interface state mediates electron-hole recombination, introduce an areal occupancy variable `N_{it}` and four primitive channels:

1. electron capture;
2. electron emission;
3. hole capture;
4. hole emission.

The boundary state vector is then

\[
x_c=(n_b,p_b,N_{it}).
\]

Eliminating `N_it` generally produces:

- frequency-dependent effective surface admittance;
- colored reduced covariance;
- electron-hole cross correlations;
- an additional interface pole;
- possible apparent multimode or power-law behavior when multiple interface states are present.

A constant deterministic `S` is the zero-frequency reduction of this model only under a controlled time-scale separation.

## 18. Relation to a measured surface recombination velocity

An experimentally inferred `S` can absorb:

- interface-state density;
- capture cross sections;
- band bending;
- carrier statistics;
- excitation level;
- contact geometry;
- transport through a barrier layer;
- interfacial capacitance;
- the measurement bandwidth.

Therefore `S` is not automatically an intrinsic stochastic parameter. R06 must label every `S` as one of:

1. microscopic exchange velocity;
2. low-frequency effective pair-recombination velocity;
3. geometry-conditioned fit parameter;
4. passivated free-surface velocity;
5. metal-contact transfer parameter.

## 19. Numerical acceptance tests

### C1 — local detailed balance

Verify

\[
\ln(a^{out}/a^{in})=\Delta\mu/(k_BT)
\]

over the declared operating range.

### C2 — event/Onsager equivalence

At equilibrium verify

\[
a^{out}+a^{in}=2k_BT K_c.
\]

### C3 — covariance positivity

Every primitive contact covariance block must be positive semidefinite to numerical tolerance.

### C4 — equilibrium boundary variance

For the isolated boundary cell, verify

\[
\operatorname{Var}(N_b)=c_{eq}V_b
\]

in the classical limit.

### C5 — state-terminal cross covariance

Removing the off-diagonal state-terminal block must measurably violate the equilibrium terminal FDT test. The full model must restore it.

### C6 — blocking limit

As `S -> 0`, verify vanishing particle flux and exchange-source activity, and identify any emerging null mode.

### C7 — reservoir limit

As `S -> infinity`, verify:

- boundary density PSD tends to zero at fixed frequency;
- integrated instantaneous variance remains thermodynamically correct;
- the complete loaded terminal PSD converges;
- source components are not interpreted separately when singular.

### C8 — mesh scaling

Under `h -> h/2`, pointwise density-source intensity scales as required while integrated population covariance and terminal PSD converge.

### C9 — contact-model hierarchy

Compare:

1. deterministic Robin boundary with no contact source;
2. independent exchange event model;
3. pair-recombination boundary;
4. dynamic interface-state model;
5. thermionic-emission contact where applicable.

Report the reduction error in terminal PSD, responsivity, and fitted corner frequency.

### C10 — generalized statistics

Replace `c_eq/(k_BT)` by `chi_c`; quantify the error made by the Boltzmann approximation for the selected HgCdTe state.

## 20. Current conclusion

The forward/reverse event model is exactly consistent with linear Onsager FDT for a classical finite-velocity contact and generalizes through the equilibrium susceptibility `chi_c`.

The general stochastic-interface concept is likely established prior art. The R06 value lies in conservation-consistent implementation, controlled reduction between interface models, coupling to bipolar HgCdTe bulk/trap dynamics, and quantitative error maps—not in claiming the existence of contact fluctuations.
