# Complete quadratic invariant space for the CdTe eight-band basis

## Status

The conventional homogeneous Kane quadratic block does not close the static CdTe matrices, but the complete `Td`-covariant and time-reversal-even quadratic matrix space does.

This is a group-theoretical and matrix-closure result at the existing unconverged static smoke point. It is not a converged CdTe parameter result and is not yet novelty-audited.

## Construction

Write the homogeneous quadratic Hamiltonian as

```text
H2(k) = sum_a q_a(k) M_a,
```

where `q_a` spans the six real symmetric quadratic monomials and `M_a` is Hermitian in the fixed Novik

```text
Gamma6 + Gamma8 + Gamma7
```

basis.

The coefficient space has real dimension

```text
6 x 64 = 384.
```

Project this space onto functions satisfying

```text
D(g) H2(k) D(g)^dagger = H2(g k)
```

for all 24 spatial operations of `Td`, together with time-reversal evenness

```text
T H2(k)* T^dagger = H2(-k) = H2(k).
```

The resulting Reynolds projector is Hermitian and idempotent to approximately `1e-14`. Its trace is exactly 10 within numerical precision.

## Interpretation

The approximately 49% failure of the conventional quadratic Kane block is a model-form failure at this static smoke point. It is not caused by:

- loss of the selected subspace;
- finite-radius contamination;
- a rank-one matrix defect;
- poor parameter conditioning;
- failure of cubic or time-reversal symmetry.

The complete symmetry-allowed quadratic space reproduces the projected matrices essentially to the numerical precision of the extraction.

This does not imply that six newly discovered physical parameters are required. The six combinations may correspond wholly or partly to known complete zincblende `k.p` invariants, remote-band downfolding terms, or basis conventions omitted by the reduced homogeneous Kane form.
