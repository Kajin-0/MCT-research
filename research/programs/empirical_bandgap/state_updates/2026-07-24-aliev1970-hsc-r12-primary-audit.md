# R01 state update: Aliev et al. 1970 HSC_R12 primary-source audit

**Date:** 2026-07-24  
**Issue:** #377  
**Source:** HSC_R12

The complete four-page primary article was recovered and audited.

The source is strong-field thermomagnetic transport at 300 K, not magneto-optical spectroscopy. Eleven Table 1 effective-mass records were reconstructed for polycrystalline n-type material over `0.05 <= x <= 0.20`.

The decisive provenance result is that the Table 1 signed-gap values are rounded evaluations of the adopted relation

```text
epsilon_g(x,T)=-0.3+5e-4*T+[1.91-1e-3*T]*x eV
```

attributed by the source to Wiley and Dexter. They are model inputs, not independent Aliev gap measurements. The repeated `x=0.10` rows therefore do not create repeated gap evidence.

Figure 1 supports only a qualitative statement that the gap and band-edge mass near `x=0.10` are nearly zero at 300 K; no separate numerical intercept-derived gap or uncertainty is printed.

```text
controlling decision
primary_source_recovered_thermomagnetic_effective_mass_table_reconstructed_gap_values_are_adopted_wiley_dexter_equation_inputs_hansen_marker_mapping_unresolved
```

Hansen's exact HSC_R12 marker mapping remains unresolved. Any common benchmark must prevent this source from being counted as independent evidence for the Wiley-Dexter gap relation or as independent validation of Hansen.
