# HgCdTe absorption observation-model bias benchmark

**Date:** 2026-07-19  
**Status:** validated synthetic method result  
**Decision:** measurement definition must be treated as an explicit uncertainty source; no universal edge correction is authorized.

## Question

Can published absorption-model structures generate edge shifts comparable to the existing approximately `+9.04 meV` Chu/provisional cross-source residual without changing the latent HgCdTe material-gap equation?

## Scope and claim boundary

This is a formula-level synthetic benchmark. The repository's provisional gap defines a latent reference edge for four representative composition/temperature scenarios. No experimental curve is fitted, no specimen is diagnosed, and the source-inspired nonparabolic curve is not promoted as a material model.

The benchmark uses:

1. the Moazzami fractional-power form

```text
alpha(E) = K(x,T) * (E-Eg)^n(x,T) / E
```

with the published `K(x,T)` and `n(x,T)` coefficients from DOI `10.1007/s11664-005-0019-3`;

2. a smooth Urbach plus hyperbolic nonparabolic reference motivated by the band-edge structure discussed in DOI `10.1063/1.2245220`;

3. free-, parabolic- (`p=0.5`), and linear- (`p=1`) power-law edge fits;

4. fixed absorption thresholds from `600` to `2000 cm^-1`;

5. a 36-case sensitivity sweep over Urbach energies `4`, `6`, and `10 meV` and edge absorption amplitudes `200`, `400`, and `800 cm^-1`.

## Correct-model control

When synthetic data are generated by the Moazzami fractional-power law and fitted with a free fractional exponent, the recovered edge bias is approximately `+0.00375 meV` in every scenario. The fitted exponent agrees with the generating exponent within the declared `0.003` tolerance.

This control proves that the deterministic grid fit itself does not generate the multi-meV shifts.

## Exponent mismatch

Forcing the same Moazzami curves to a linear exponent produces edge biases of:

```text
x=0.21, 80 K   -8.09875 meV
x=0.31, 80 K   -4.85250 meV
x=0.21, 300 K  -0.55625 meV
x=0.31, 300 K  -0.96750 meV
```

The size and temperature dependence are model-specific. A single fixed exponent cannot be assumed to produce a constant source correction.

## Nonparabolic/Urbach model mismatch

Fitting the source-inspired nonparabolic reference with a free power law still moves the inferred edge by:

```text
x=0.21, 80 K   -11.36250 meV
x=0.31, 80 K   -15.34375 meV
x=0.21, 300 K  -13.68125 meV
x=0.31, 300 K  -15.94750 meV
```

Allowing the exponent to float is therefore not sufficient protection against an incorrect functional family or a tail-contaminated fit window.

## Fixed-threshold edge bias

For the nominal Urbach/nonparabolic reference, defining the edge at `alpha=1500 cm^-1` gives positive biases of:

```text
x=0.21, 80 K     9.20881 meV
x=0.31, 80 K    15.78717 meV
x=0.21, 300 K   13.33875 meV
x=0.31, 300 K   16.77282 meV
```

Changing only the threshold from `600` to `2000 cm^-1` spans:

```text
18.62299 to 29.33460 meV
```

across the four nominal scenarios.

The existing Chu/provisional mean signed discrepancy is `+9.03919 meV`. The nominal `1500 cm^-1` threshold benchmark has the same positive sign and begins at `+9.20881 meV`. This is evidence of scale compatibility, not a diagnosis of the Chu measurements.

## Sensitivity sweep

Across 36 Urbach/amplitude cases:

```text
threshold 1000 cm^-1: median bias  4.60726 meV
threshold 1500 cm^-1: median bias 13.06082 meV
threshold 2000 cm^-1: median bias 24.74825 meV
```

The `1500 cm^-1` range is `3.75865-21.96533 meV`; the effect is not a universal constant.

## Scientific decision

The benchmark establishes:

- absorption threshold and model family can generate multi-meV inferred-edge shifts without changing the latent material gap;
- the sign and scale can overlap the existing cross-source discrepancy;
- a floating power exponent does not eliminate model-family bias;
- a fixed source correction is not defensible because the bias depends on composition, temperature, tail parameters, fit window, and threshold.

Therefore:

- production observation correction: **not authorized**;
- static material-gap refit: **not authorized**;
- measurement model and threshold must be preserved in every gap record;
- an edge-model uncertainty envelope must accompany future absorption-derived gaps.

## Required reporting contract

Future absorption-derived gap records must include:

1. measurement modality;
2. explicit absorption model;
3. fit energy or absorption window;
4. fixed threshold, if used;
5. carrier type and density when available;
6. Urbach/tail treatment;
7. model-family or threshold sensitivity interval.
