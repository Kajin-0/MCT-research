# Decision record: cross-material prior art for Gaussian-tail curvature

**Date:** 2026-07-22  
**Program:** R03 — distributional band-edge observables  
**Controlling issue:** #251  
**Related work:** #225 / PR #226; #235 / PR #237

## Decision

**Narrow the R03 Gaussian-tail claim and preserve the theorem as a candidate operator-specific result. Do not authorize a manuscript.**

The cross-material audit establishes that much of the motivating phenomenology and model structure is prior art:

- correlated Gaussian disorder can produce a broad apparently exponential interval;
- a deeper Gaussian tail can follow an intermediate Urbach-like crossover;
- Gaussian averaging of direct-gap square-root absorption is established;
- the inverse logarithmic slope is already used as an energy-dependent apparent Urbach energy;
- narrow fitting windows can force arbitrary exponential fits;
- generic partial moments and recurrence relations are standard mathematics;
- asymptotic tail class depends on the fluctuation kernel, and non-Gaussian kernels can produce a true exponential asymptote.

These elements must not be presented as new.

## Retained candidate contribution

The only retained candidate distinction is the assembled result for

$$
\alpha_p(E)=A\,\mathbb{E}[(E-G)_+^p],
\qquad
G\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu_G,\sigma_G^2),
\qquad p\ge0:
$$

1. exact operator-specific derivative identities for arbitrary `p`;
2. log-concavity of the convolved absorption;
3. monotonic increase of the local inverse logarithmic slope;
4. normalized deep-tail logarithmic curvature approaching `-1`;
5. fixed-dynamic-range best-affine residual decreasing as `O(|z_upper|^-2)`;
6. the HgCdTe publication-figure consequence that curvature is not recoverable without an independent standardized gap-location or above-gap amplitude anchor.

The candidate contribution is the **combination and measurement consequence**, not the generic partial-moment recurrence, Gaussian convolution, apparent Urbach energy, or finite-window fitting problem individually.

## Evidence boundary

Primary full text was inspected for the John et al. 1988 and Kaiser et al. 2021 sources, and accessible primary text was inspected for the Gokmen and Guerra works. Publisher abstracts and a first-party thesis record were available for the two O'Leary 1995 papers, but their article full texts were not recovered in this workstream.

Therefore the correct conclusion is:

> No equivalent assembled theorem was located in the inspected primary-source material.

The following conclusion is not authorized:

> No equivalent theorem exists in the literature.

The unresolved O'Leary full texts remain a material novelty-audit gap because those papers directly average local density or joint-density-of-states functions over Gaussian band-potential fluctuations.

## Consequence for active work

No further theorem implementation or synthetic parameter sweep is authorized from this audit. The current analytical and recoverability branches already contain the decision-changing mathematical content.

The next useful work is restricted to:

1. retrieving and auditing the O'Leary 1995 article full texts, or recording a source-unavailable decision after reasonable attempts;
2. finding numerical absorption data with uncertainty or covariance;
3. finding an independent mean-gap, intrinsic-amplitude, or above-gap branch constraint;
4. applying the recoverability gate before any spectrum digitization or fitting.

## Manuscript consequence

No manuscript is authorized.

A narrow theorem paper could become viable only after:

- the remaining close full-text precedents are resolved;
- a public-data or source-bounded falsification path exists;
- the claim is framed as an observation-operator and identifiability result rather than a new microscopic theory of Urbach tails;
- the HgCdTe application is externally anchored rather than inferred from visual straightness.

## Claims withdrawn from novelty consideration

- Gaussian-disorder production of an apparently exponential interval;
- crossover from an Urbach-like interval to a deeper Gaussian tail;
- Gaussian convolution of a square-root direct-gap edge;
- energy dependence of apparent Urbach energy;
- fit-window dependence of exponential tail parameters;
- generic partial-moment recurrence relations;
- any universal statement that disorder convolution must have a Gaussian deep tail.

## Claims retained only as bounded candidates

- the assembled all-`p` differential, curvature, asymptotic, and finite-window theorem for the declared operator;
- the explicit `O(|z_upper|^-2)` recoverability scaling;
- the source-conditioned no-digitization decision for the Finkman HgCdTe figures.

## Stop rule

Terminate the R03 Gaussian-tail manuscript path if either of the following occurs:

- a close primary source is found that states an equivalent assembled theorem and recoverability result;
- no numerical or independently anchored spectrum can test the result beyond synthetic model behavior.

Until then, preserve the work as an active scoped result with no manuscript authorization.
