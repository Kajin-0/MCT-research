## Source-established results

### Ideal semiconductor interface

For one activated exchange process, the mean interface flux is the difference of forward and reverse activities and the covariance is their sum.

The source derives:

- a nonlinear stochastic boundary condition;
- a generalized fluctuation relation;
- ordinary Onsager-FDT in the linear limit;
- an ideal metal-semiconductor specialization;
- shot-to-thermal crossover.

### Nonideal interface

Interface states are represented by multiple primitive activated exchange processes. Composite interface currents have nonzero cross covariance whenever they share a primitive event channel.

The covariance is therefore generated by an incidence/stoichiometry matrix acting on independent primitive event activities.

### Schottky contact coupled to a device

The Schottky source combines:

- drift-diffusion-Poisson transport;
- thermionic-emission boundary noise;
- distributed diffusion noise;
- impedance-field transfer;
- equilibrium Nyquist verification;
- diffusion, thermionic, and mixed transport regimes.

The contact source obeys

\[
S_{I_c}=2q(I+2I_c)
\]

in the source convention. At equilibrium it produces the contact contribution `4 k_B T R_c`.

The depletion-series cross term is also required for local Nyquist recovery.

## Interpretation correction

The Schottky paper assumes independent primitive thermionic and bulk diffusion sources. Its reported cross term is produced by a shared distributed bulk source appearing in two output transfer components.

R06 must distinguish:

1. primitive event covariance;
2. state/terminal cross covariance from one primitive event;
3. output-component covariance produced by a shared source and different transfer functions;
4. genuine primitive interface-bulk correlations, if a more microscopic model derives them.

## Novelty decision

The following are not novel R06 contributions:

- stochastic semiconductor-interface boundary conditions;
- forward/reverse contact-event covariance;
- contact Onsager-FDT;
- thermionic-emission contact noise;
- interface-state-mediated noise;
- cross covariance from shared interface events;
- Schottky contact noise coupled to drift-diffusion-Poisson transport;
- equilibrium Nyquist recovery from surface, bulk, and cross terms.

The stochastic-contact novelty hypothesis is therefore **closed negatively**.

## Reframed R06 objective

R06 proceeds under the following objective:

> Quantify, with controlled numerical and asymptotic error bounds, when established semiconductor-interface fluctuation theory can be reduced to quasineutral, deterministic finite-velocity, one-carrier, or single-lifetime models in a finite bipolar HgCdTe photoconductor.

The principal outputs are errors in:

- DC current and differential resistance;
- responsivity;
- terminal current and voltage PSD;
- fitted corner frequency;
- inferred microscopic lifetime;
- noise-equivalent power and detectivity;
- regime classification.

## Candidate paper-level contribution

A paper objective remains viable only if the project establishes at least one of:

1. an analytical or asymptotic error bound for eliminating a dynamic interface state;
2. a dimensionless boundary for replacing a stochastic interface by deterministic `S`;
3. a quantitative boundary for quasineutral ambipolar reduction of the full bipolar Poisson spectrum;
4. an uncertainty-robust HgCdTe regime map showing when fitted corner frequency fails as a lifetime estimator;
5. a falsifiable prediction distinguishing contact, trap, screening, and diffusion modes in terminal spectra.
