# Seiler et al. (1990) — acquisition dossier

## Bibliographic record

D. G. Seiler, J. R. Lowney, C. L. Littler, and M. R. Loloee, “Temperature and composition dependence of the energy gap of Hg$_{1-x}$Cd$_x$Te by two-photon magnetoabsorption techniques,” *Journal of Vacuum Science & Technology A* **8**, 1237 (1990), DOI `10.1116/1.576952`.

The title, journal, volume, first page, and DOI are corroborated by the AIP metadata page embedded with the acquired Hansen paper. The complete page range and issue remain pending primary-paper inspection.

## Why this source is high priority

The study is a post-Hansen, pre-Laurenti/modern-magnetospectroscopy measurement using two-photon magnetoabsorption. It may provide:

- a more direct band-edge observable than detector cutoff;
- temperature series on fixed specimens;
- composition-dependent signed or algebraic gap information;
- an independent test of Hansen and Laurenti;
- a historical bridge to Krishnamurthy’s 1995 electron–phonon comparison.

It is therefore more valuable for held-out model discrimination than another equation compiled from the same historical sources.

## Required extraction after acquisition

For every specimen, record:

1. sample identifier and material form;
2. growth method and substrate;
3. reported Cd composition and determination method;
4. composition uncertainty, gradient, and any post-fit recalibration;
5. strain state and carrier concentration if reported;
6. temperature points and temperature uncertainty;
7. magnetic-field range and optical geometry;
8. two-photon transition assignment;
9. exact definition and sign convention of $E_g$;
10. fitted band parameters and whether momentum matrix elements were fixed;
11. reported statistical and systematic uncertainty;
12. all table values and calibrated figure coordinates;
13. whether multiple temperatures belong to the same specimen;
14. which empirical equations are compared or fitted.

## Model-discrimination value

The paper will be considered decisive only to the extent that composition is independently measured. Near the inversion transition,

$$
\sigma_{E,x}\approx
\left|\frac{\partial E_g}{\partial x}\right|\sigma_x,
$$

and a nominal third-decimal composition label can produce several meV of apparent model error.

The preferred use is therefore a within-specimen temperature-shape test, followed by a composition-marginalized absolute-gap comparison.

## Stop rule

If the paper reports only model-derived compositions or no reconstructable temperature-series values, it remains useful prior art but should not be promoted to a high-weight independent benchmark dataset.
