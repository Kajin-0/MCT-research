# Gomila, Bulashenko, and Rubí (1998) equation-level audit

**Source:** G. Gomila, O. M. Bulashenko, and J. M. Rubí, “Local noise analysis of a Schottky contact: Combined thermionic-emission–diffusion theory,” *Journal of Applied Physics* **83**, 2619–2630 (1998).  
**DOI:** `10.1063/1.367024`  
**Verification status:** user-supplied full publisher paper inspected at equation level.

## 1. Scope

The paper develops a low-frequency analytical noise model for an ideal n-type Schottky diode. It combines:

- drift-diffusion transport in the semiconductor;
- Poisson electrostatics and a depletion layer;
- thermionic emission at the metal-semiconductor interface;
- a distributed bulk diffusion-current noise source;
- a stochastic thermionic-emission boundary source;
- an impedance-field decomposition of terminal voltage noise;
- equilibrium Nyquist verification;
- diffusion-limited, thermionic-emission-limited, and intermediate regimes.

The paper does not treat a bipolar photoconductor, generation-recombination traps, optical generation, or a full-frequency spectrum beyond the low-frequency plateau.

## 2. Deterministic model

The full current equation includes drift, diffusion, and displacement current:

\[
\frac{I(t)}{A}
=qnv(E)+qD(E)\frac{\partial n}{\partial x}
+\varepsilon\frac{\partial E}{\partial t}.
\]

Poisson electrostatics is

\[
\frac{\partial E}{\partial x}
=\frac{q}{\varepsilon}(N_D-n).
\]

The thermionic-emission boundary is

\[
J(0,t)=q[n_0(t)-n_0^{eq}]v_rA.
\]

This is explicitly interpreted as the difference between semiconductor-to-metal and metal-to-semiconductor carrier fluxes.

The low-frequency noise calculation assumes times much longer than the dielectric relaxation time, so displacement current is neglected in the reduced fluctuation operator. This limitation must not be transferred to the full-frequency R06 solver.

## 3. Dimensionless contact regime

The source introduces

\[
\beta_c=\frac{\mu E_{th}}{v_r},
\qquad
E_{th}=\frac{k_BT}{qL_D},
\]

where `v_r` is the thermionic-emission velocity.

The regimes are:

- `beta_c << 1`: diffusion-limited;
- `beta_c >> 1`: thermionic-emission-limited;
- `beta_c ~ 1`: combined thermionic-emission-diffusion control.

It also uses the barrier/depletion ratio

\[
\alpha_c=\frac{n_0^{eq}}{N_D}.
\]

For R06, `beta_c` is a contact-to-Debye-layer transport ratio distinct from the end-contact Biot number `SL/D` used in a finite photoconductor. Both may be required in a microscopic contact model.

## 4. Primitive noise sources

### 4.1 Bulk diffusion-current source

The local bulk source is

\[
K(x)=4q^2n(x)D(x)
\]

in the paper’s one-sided per-hertz convention and low-heating approximation.

The distributed current sources are delta correlated in position and are treated as Markovian at the macroscopic time scale.

### 4.2 Thermionic-emission boundary source

The surface source spectral density is

\[
S_{I_c}=2q[I+2I_c],
\]

with

\[
I_c=qn_0^{eq}v_rA.
\]

The sign of `I` follows the paper’s current orientation. The formula has the forward-plus-reverse structure:

- at equilibrium `I=0`, `S_{I_c}=4qI_c`;
- when one direction dominates, `S_{I_c}` tends to `2q|I|`.

This is the Schottky specialization of the ideal-interface result derived in Gomila and Rubí, *Physica A* 258 (1998).

The surface source enters the fluctuating boundary condition, not as a post hoc terminal source.

## 5. Terminal transfer and decomposition

The terminal voltage fluctuation is written as

\[
\delta V
=Z_c\delta I_s
+\int_0^L \nabla Z(x)\delta I_x\,dx,
\]

where `Z_c` weights the thermionic surface source and the bulk impedance field is decomposed as

\[
\nabla Z=\nabla Z_{dep}+\nabla Z_{ser}.
\]

The total impedance is

\[
Z=Z_c+Z_{dep}+Z_{ser}.
\]

The voltage-noise PSD is decomposed into

\[
S_V=S_{Vc}+S_{Vdep}+S_{Vser}+S_{Vcros}.
\]

The cross term is

\[
S_{Vcros}
=A\int_0^L
2\nabla Z_{dep}(x)\nabla Z_{ser}(x)K(x)\,dx.
\]

### Important interpretation

`S_Vcros` is generated by the same distributed bulk diffusion source appearing in two transfer components. It is not a primitive covariance between the thermionic surface source and the bulk source; those primitive sources are assumed independent in this paper.

This corrects an overly broad shorthand such as “bulk/contact source cross correlation.” The established result is a terminal cross term between depletion/junction and series voltage contributions after propagation through a common bulk source and electrostatic response.

R06 must preserve this distinction:

1. primitive event covariance;
2. transfer-induced covariance between output components;
3. possible true primitive interface/bulk correlations in more microscopic models.

## 6. Equilibrium Nyquist verification

At equilibrium the contact resistance is

\[
R_c=Z_c
=\frac{k_BT}{q^2n_0^{eq}v_rA}
=\frac{k_BT}{qI_c}.
\]

The contact noise satisfies

\[
R_c^2S_{I_c}=4k_BTR_c.
\]

For the bulk, the source derives local contributions

\[
s_{Vdep}^{eq}
=4k_BT
\left(\frac{r(x)}{r(0)}\right)^2R(x),
\]

\[
s_{Vser}^{eq}
=4k_BT
\left(1-\frac{r(x)}{r(0)}\right)^2R(x),
\]

\[
s_{Vcros}^{eq}
=8k_BT
\left(1-\frac{r(x)}{r(0)}\right)
\frac{r(x)}{r(0)}R(x).
\]

Their sum is

\[
s_V^{eq}(x)=4k_BTR(x).
\]

The paper explicitly states that the cross term is necessary to recover the local Nyquist result.

The complete equilibrium terminal result is therefore

\[
S_V^{eq}=4k_BT
\left[
R_c+\int_0^LR(x)\,dx
\right].
\]

## 7. Equivalent-circuit consequence

The source compares its result with the conventional Schottky equivalent-noise circuit and identifies:

- `R_j = Z_c + Z_dep`;
- `R_s = Z_ser`;
- junction-associated noise from `S_Vc + S_Vdep`;
- series thermal noise from `S_Vser`;
- an additional `S_Vcros` term missing from the conventional uncorrelated circuit.

The equivalent circuit is therefore a limiting reduction, not an exact decomposition into independent noise generators.

## 8. Bias dependence

The source analyzes three regimes controlled by `beta_c` and finds that:

- at low bias, the depletion/junction region dominates;
- near intermediate bias, depletion and series regions overlap strongly and the cross term can be important;
- at high forward current, the depletion layer collapses and series resistance dominates;
- similar I-V behavior can arise from diffusion-limited and thermionic-limited transport, so noise and known contact parameters are needed to distinguish mechanisms.

The precise numerical curves use a high barrier `alpha_c=10^-7`, a sample length `L=15L_D`, and constant mobility and Einstein diffusion.

These are benchmark conditions, not HgCdTe material values.

## 9. What is established prior art

R06 cannot claim novelty for:

- a stochastic thermionic-emission boundary source;
- the forward-plus-reverse Schottky contact-noise formula;
- combining thermionic and diffusion noise in one drift-diffusion-Poisson framework;
- impedance-field propagation of a surface source;
- junction/series output cross terms;
- equilibrium Nyquist recovery from contact, bulk, and cross contributions;
- diffusion-, thermionic-, and mixed contact regimes;
- deriving an equivalent circuit as a controlled limit of a field model.

## 10. Mandatory R06 benchmarks

1. Reproduce `S_Ic=2q(I+2I_c)` after convention translation.
2. At equilibrium recover `R_c=k_BT/(qI_c)` and `R_c^2S_Ic=4k_BTR_c`.
3. Reproduce the local bulk identity `s_Vdep+s_Vser+s_Vcros=4k_BTR(x)`.
4. Show that deleting `S_Vcros` produces a nonzero equilibrium FDT error.
5. Recover the `beta_c << 1`, `beta_c >> 1`, and `beta_c ~ 1` transport ordering.
6. Compare direct resolvent and impedance-field evaluations of the Schottky terminal PSD.
7. Verify that primitive surface and bulk sources remain independent in this benchmark while output components are correlated through transfer decomposition.

## 11. Novelty consequence

The stochastic-contact novelty hypothesis is closed negatively.

The retained R06 contribution must be a reduction or extension result, for example:

- quantify the error from replacing an interface-event model by deterministic `S` in a bipolar HgCdTe photoconductor;
- determine when interface-state elimination creates colored effective boundary noise;
- identify when quasineutral or one-carrier Schottky reductions fail under HgCdTe conditions;
- map how contact, diffusion, trap, dielectric, and circuit modes alter inferred lifetime and detectivity.

## 12. Limitations relative to R06

- unipolar Schottky diode rather than bipolar photoconductor;
- low-frequency plateau only;
- no generation-recombination trap population;
- no optical generation;
- long sample `L >> L_D` and one ideal ohmic far contact;
- thermionic emission only, without pair surface recombination;
- nondegenerate Einstein transport in the numerical examples;
- no HgCdTe-specific material uncertainty analysis.
