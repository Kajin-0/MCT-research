## 1. Fluctuations generated at semiconductor interfaces

**Authors:** G. Gomila and J. M. Rubí  
**Venue:** *Physica A* **258**, 17–31 (1998)  
**PII:** `S0378-4371(98)00199-X`  
**DOI:** `10.1016/S0378-4371(98)00199-X`  
**Verification:** user-supplied full paper; equation-level audit complete

### Established results

For an ideal activated interface process,

\[
J=\lambda_{12}z_1-\lambda_{21}z_2+\xi,
\]

with

\[
\langle\xi(t)\xi(t')\rangle
=
\frac{\lambda_{12}z_1+\lambda_{21}z_2}{A}
\delta(t-t').
\]

Thus the mean is the forward-minus-reverse activity and the covariance is the forward-plus-reverse activity.

The paper also derives:

- a nonlinear generalized fluctuation relation involving `coth(beta Delta F/2)`;
- ordinary Onsager-FDT recovery in the linear regime;
- an ideal metal-semiconductor stochastic boundary;
- nonideal interfaces with explicit interface-state exchange;
- nonzero cross covariance between composite interface currents sharing a primitive interface-state transition.

### R06 consequence

The general stochastic contact covariance, interface-state extension, and shared-event cross-covariance structure are established prior art. They are not candidate novelty.

The R06 event-stoichiometry construction is retained as the correct implementation architecture because it reproduces the source theory automatically.

Detailed audit: `source_notes/gomila_rubi_1998_interfaces.md`.

## 2. Local noise analysis of a Schottky contact

**Authors:** G. Gomila, O. M. Bulashenko, and J. M. Rubí  
**Venue:** *Journal of Applied Physics* **83**, 2619–2630 (1998)  
**DOI:** `10.1063/1.367024`  
**Verification:** user-supplied full publisher paper; equation-level audit complete

### Established results

The source combines:

- semiconductor drift-diffusion and Poisson electrostatics;
- thermionic-emission mean boundary conditions;
- distributed bulk diffusion noise;
- a stochastic thermionic-emission boundary source;
- impedance-field terminal transfer;
- depletion, series, and cross contributions;
- equilibrium Nyquist verification;
- thermal-to-shot crossover.

The thermionic source is

\[
S_{I_c}=2q(I+2I_c),
\qquad
I_c=qn_0^{eq}v_rA,
\]

in the paper’s current and one-sided spectral convention.

At equilibrium,

\[
R_c=\frac{k_BT}{qI_c},
\qquad
R_c^2S_{I_c}=4k_BTR_c.
\]

For the bulk contribution,

\[
s_{Vdep}^{eq}+s_{Vser}^{eq}+s_{Vcros}^{eq}
=4k_BTR(x).
\]

The cross term is required for Nyquist recovery.

### Interpretation correction

The paper assumes the primitive thermionic surface source and distributed bulk source are independent. Its `S_Vcros` term is generated by the same bulk source appearing in depletion and series transfer components. It is an output/transfer cross term, not a primitive surface-bulk source covariance.

R06 must distinguish primitive event covariance from cross terms generated after propagation through a shared source.

Detailed audit: `source_notes/gomila_bulashenko_rubi_1998_schottky.md`.

**DOI:** `10.1016/S0378-4371(96)00320-2`  
**Preprint:** `arXiv:cond-mat/9603066`  
**Verification:** authoritative full preprint audited

The paper supplies the deterministic thermodynamic interface scaffold:

- bulk drift-diffusion;
- surface conservation equations;
- nonlinear electrochemical-potential boundary laws;
- interface states;
- thermionic-emission specializations;

## 7. Final contact prior-art conclusion

The following are established:

- fluctuating semiconductor-interface boundary conditions;
- forward-minus-reverse mean exchange and forward-plus-reverse covariance;
- nonlinear interface fluctuation relations and linear FDT recovery;
- thermionic-emission contact noise;
- interface-state-mediated stochastic exchange;
- cross covariance between composite currents sharing interface events;
- surface-recombination GR noise;
- terminal cross terms arising from electrostatic transfer and shared distributed sources;
- equilibrium Nyquist recovery only after all required source and transfer terms are included.

Therefore R06 will not claim novelty for adding stochastic contact noise, finite surface velocity, interface states, or contact/bulk output cross terms.

## 8. Retained research contribution

The project is reframed as a controlled reduction study:

> Determine when established semiconductor-interface fluctuation theory can be reduced to a deterministic finite-velocity boundary in a bipolar HgCdTe photoconductor without corrupting terminal current noise, voltage noise, responsivity, bandwidth, or inferred lifetime beyond a declared tolerance.

Candidate outputs are:

1. dimensionless reduction-error maps;
2. fast-interface-state elimination and the resulting colored effective boundary covariance;
3. distinction between independent reservoir exchange, pair surface recombination, and interface-state mediation;
4. error in fitted corner frequency and inferred microscopic lifetime;
5. uncertainty-qualified HgCdTe regimes where a single `S` is or is not adequate.
