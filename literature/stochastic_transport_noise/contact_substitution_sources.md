## Purpose

## 1. Fluctuations generated at semiconductor interfaces

**Authors:** G. Gomila and J. M. Rubí  
**Venue:** *Physica A* **258**, 17–31 (1998)  
**DOI:** not yet verified  
**Verification:** title, authors, venue, pages, and abstract verified

The abstract states that:

- fluctuations generated by carrier exchange through a semiconductor interface are modeled explicitly;
- interfacial fluctuating terms are inserted into fluctuating boundary conditions;
- ideal and nonideal interfaces are treated;
- interface states are included;
- the derivation uses nonequilibrium thermodynamics, internal degrees of freedom, and fluctuating hydrodynamics;
- bulk and interface fluctuations are placed on the same thermodynamic footing.

### R06 consequence

This source is likely the closest prior art to the project’s stochastic-contact boundary. Until the full paper is audited, R06 must assume that the general concept and possibly the complete covariance structure are already established.

### Required extraction

1. interface state variables;
2. deterministic interfacial flux-force relations;
3. fluctuating boundary terms;
4. covariance matrix and cross terms;
5. ideal versus nonideal interface limits;
6. role of interface states;
7. equilibrium FDT statement;
8. one-/two-sided and frequency convention.

## 2. Local noise analysis of a Schottky contact

**Authors:** G. Gomila, O. M. Bulashenko, and J. M. Rubí  
**Title:** “Local noise analysis of a Schottky contact: combined thermionic-emission–diffusion theory”  
**Venue:** *Journal of Applied Physics* **83**, 2619–2630 (1998)  
**DOI:** `10.1063/1.367024`  
**Verification:** full article is reported available from the University of Barcelona repository; abstract and substantial publisher-text excerpts verified

The source treats:

- diffusion noise in the semiconductor;
- thermionic-emission noise across the metal–semiconductor interface;
- junction and series resistance contributions;
- spatially resolved local noise;
- bulk/contact cross correlation;
- Nyquist consistency at equilibrium;
- the crossover between thermal and shot-noise limits.

### R06 consequence

Independent contact and bulk noise sources cannot generally be added without their cross correlation. The same warning found in the Bulashenko impedance-field paper applies more directly to a metal–semiconductor contact.

This source substantially weakens any novelty claim based on adding an interface exchange source. It strengthens the case for a contribution based on controlled reduction to an effective finite-velocity boundary in a bipolar HgCdTe photoconductor.

### Required extraction

1. thermionic forward/reverse event rates;
2. contact source spectral density;
3. sample/contact cross-correlation term;
4. equilibrium and high-bias limits;
5. mapping from thermionic-emission velocity to an effective `S`;
6. terminal current and voltage ensembles.

## 3. Nonequilibrium thermodynamic description of junctions

**Authors:** G. Gomila and J. M. Rubí  
**Venue:** *Physica A* **234**, 851–871 (1997)  
**DOI:** `10.1016/S0378-4371(96)00320-2`  
**Preprint:** `arXiv:cond-mat/9603066`  
**Verification:** authoritative preprint metadata and abstract verified; full preprint available for later equation audit

The source derives:

- semiconductor bulk drift-diffusion equations;
- surface/interface equations;
- interface boundary conditions;
- a unified nonequilibrium thermodynamic description of bulk and junction;
- an application to metal–semiconductor junctions.

### R06 consequence

The correct contact closure should be formulated in electrochemical-potential or thermodynamic-force variables before it is reduced to `S(c-c_eq)`. A density-only Robin coefficient is generally a reduced constitutive parameter rather than a complete interface model.

## 4. Equivalent-circuit surface recombination noise

**Author:** K. S. Champlin  
**Title:** “Generation-Recombination Noise in Semiconductors—The Equivalent Circuit Approach”  
**Venue:** *IRE Transactions on Electron Devices* **7**, 29–38 (1960)  
**DOI:** `10.1109/T-ED.1960.14588`  
**Verification:** metadata and detailed abstract/summary verified

The source extends a thermal equivalent-circuit treatment from volume recombination to surface recombination. It treats spatial mode correlations and reports a diffusion-limited high-frequency `omega^-3/2` spectrum and a low-frequency amplitude correction relative to simple zero-dimensional theory.

### R06 consequence

Surface-recombination noise, correlated spatial modes, and non-Lorentzian diffusion limits are established. This source should become a surface-noise benchmark, not a novelty basis.

## 5. Diffusion and surface recombination GR spectrum

**Title:** “On the influence of diffusion and surface recombination upon the GR noise spectrum of semiconductors”  
**Venue:** *Physica* **26**, 751–760 (1960)  
**DOI:** `10.1016/0031-8914(60)90065-3`  
**Verification:** metadata and abstract verified; author list pending full text

The source criticizes earlier independent-mode treatments for neglecting correlations between spatial Fourier coefficients and develops a transmission-line treatment for volume-, surface-, and diffusion-limited regimes.

### R06 consequence

A modal decomposition must retain covariance between modes when the primitive sources or boundary processes couple them. Summing independent Lorentzians is not generally valid.

## 6. Boltzmann-Langevin source normalization

**Authors:** H. S. Min and D. Ahn  
**Title:** “Langevin noise sources for the Boltzmann transport equations with the relaxation-time approximation in nondegenerate semiconductors”  
**Venue:** *Journal of Applied Physics* **58**, 2262–2265 (1985)  
**DOI:** `10.1063/1.335943`  
**Verification:** metadata and abstract verified

The source derives intraband-scattering Langevin sources for a nondegenerate Boltzmann transport model and verifies Nyquist consistency.

### R06 consequence

This is useful as an independent check on the diffusion-current source normalization and on the regime in which a local drift-diffusion fluctuation source is valid.

## 7. Current prior-art conclusion

Based on the supplied papers plus this adjacent-source triage:

- deterministic finite contact velocity is established;
- surface-recombination GR noise is established;
- thermionic-emission contact noise is established;
- fluctuating semiconductor-interface boundary conditions are likely established;
- bulk/contact cross correlations are established;
- interface-state-mediated fluctuations are likely established.

Therefore R06 should not claim novelty for a stochastic contact source itself.

The strongest remaining contribution is a verified and uncertainty-qualified reduction study showing when established interface-noise theory may be replaced by a simpler finite-velocity boundary in a bipolar HgCdTe photoconductor without corrupting terminal noise, responsivity, or inferred lifetime.
