# Strengthened manuscript scope and controlling claims

## Recommended title

**Extraction-model and fit-domain dependence of fitted HgCdTe optical intercepts: a two-spectrum case study**

This record supersedes broader title and claim language in earlier working drafts. The current scientific-review manuscript uses **fitted optical intercept** rather than treating every fitted parameter as a directly measured physical band gap.

## Evidence boundary

The evidence consists of two 300 K absorption-coefficient curves reconstructed from the published Moazzami et al. Figure 6 bitmaps. Those curves are themselves outputs of the source spectroscopic-ellipsometry inversion. The analysis therefore quantifies downstream reconstruction and extraction dependence conditional on the published curves; it does not estimate the total experimental uncertainty of the source measurements or identify a unique physical gap.

## Primary estimand class

The primary comparison is restricted to fitted-intercept models:

- fractional-power fit with free exponent;
- fixed exponents 0.5, 0.7, source-panel exponent, and 1;
- the provenance-bound Chu Kane-region form.

Fixed-absorption crossings are operational coordinates and are excluded from the fitted-intercept span.

Boundary-limited candidates remain in the machine record but are excluded from the primary span.

## Immediately controlled robustness analyses

1. coherent calibration-corner sensitivity;
2. raw centerline, unweighted isotonic, weighted isotonic, and source-line-envelope reconstructions;
3. fixed versus reconstruction-specific fit membership;
4. fail-closed exclusion of scenarios that violate the predeclared edge-search domain;
5. every contiguous one-, three-, and five-point omission;
6. a predeclared lower/upper absorption-endpoint grid crossed with uniform-point, energy-interval, and log-absorption-interval weighting;
7. energy-resolved residual records for every nominal fitted model;
8. a 4 by 3 sensitivity grid for the descriptive line-envelope compatibility screen.

The residual screen is secondary and nonprobabilistic. Its retained span changes under stricter coverage thresholds and is not used as a physical confidence interval or abstract-level headline.

## Principal numerical results

| Quantity | x = 0.226 | x = 0.310 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Boundary-excluded nominal fitted-model span | 5.09 meV | 6.68 meV |
| Fixed-membership reconstruction maximum | 0.69 meV | 0.81 meV |
| All-admissible reconstruction maximum | 1.25 meV | 1.96 meV |
| Maximum endpoint/weighting displacement | 5.71 meV | 5.44 meV |
| Non-boundary span across endpoint/weighting grid | 2.59-11.00 meV | 1.84-11.09 meV |

The two-point reconstruction irregularity near 0.198 eV changes a non-boundary fitted intercept by approximately 0.12 meV under exact omission and does not control the multi-meV dependence. Its physical origin remains unidentified and the nominal reconstruction is not corrected.

## Authorized conclusion

For these two reconstructed, model-derived spectra, the fitted HgCdTe optical intercept is materially conditioned by extraction model and especially by fit-domain endpoints and weighting convention.

## Prohibited conclusions

The present record does not authorize:

- a universal 5-7 meV HgCdTe band-gap uncertainty;
- a unique or corrected physical gap;
- a probabilistic measurement-uncertainty budget;
- a preferred universal empirical gap equation;
- a physical interpretation of the local plateau;
- generalization to other temperatures, laboratories, specimens, or measurement modalities without additional evidence.
