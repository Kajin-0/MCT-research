**Re:** Research Paper, “Observation-model uncertainty and identifiability in HgCdTe band-gap extraction”

Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript for publication as a Research Paper in *Infrared Physics & Technology*.

The manuscript addresses a practical limitation in meV-scale HgCdTe band-gap comparisons: an absorption-derived edge depends on the observation definition and specimen state, not only on the latent material gap. We apply a fail-closed extraction ensemble to two calibrated real infrared spectroscopic ellipsometry spectra. Admissible fitted observation models shift the extracted edges by 6.414–6.830 meV, whereas the declared digitization-coordinate uncertainty shifts them by no more than 0.891 meV. Fixed absorption definitions also reverse the nominal closest material-gap relation. The published Seiler relation is nominally closest for fitted-model edges, but its 0.177–0.255 meV advantage over Hansen is not identified without specimen-level composition uncertainty.

The work combines infrared material characterization, optical spectroscopy, semiconductor physics, validated modeling, and a concrete experimental design. Rather than proposing another empirical coefficient set, it defines a reproducible observation-model framework and an audit-grade paired 2 × 2 × 2 acquisition design for separating latent-gap, carrier, vacancy, and method contributions.

All derived spectra, calibration records, code, tables, deterministic figures, machine-readable decisions, and integrity manifests required to reproduce the results are available in the associated repository. Copyrighted source pages and figures are not redistributed. The paper does not claim a universal absorption correction, a selected production edge, or a universal replacement for Hansen.
