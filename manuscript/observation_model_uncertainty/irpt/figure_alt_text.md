## Figure 1

Logarithmic absorption coefficient versus photon energy for the digitized Moazzami 2005 Figure 6a IRSE spectrum at composition `x=0.226` and 300 K. The measured curve rises from approximately `10^2` to above `10^3 cm^-1` across the absorption edge. Six fitted observation-model curves are overlaid: free fractional power, fixed fractional powers, the source-panel exponent, and the Chu 1994 Kane-region form. The fitted edges span 181.086-187.500 meV. Boundary-limited candidates are retained but no fitted curve is selected as the latent material gap.

## Figure 2

Two-panel comparison of extracted edge energy for the `x=0.226` and `x=0.310` specimens. Each panel lists fitted fractional-power and Chu candidates followed by fixed absorption crossings from 400 to 5000 `cm^-1`. Horizontal reference lines show Hansen, published Seiler, Laurenti, and provisional Hansen-Pade predictions. Open markers identify boundary-limited fits. The fitted-model candidates form a 6.414 meV span for `x=0.226` and a 6.830 meV span for `x=0.310`; higher fixed thresholds extend far above those fitted-model envelopes.

## Figure 3

Residual intervals, in meV, between extracted edges and four material-gap comparators for the two specimens. Narrow dark intervals represent the fitted observation-model envelope. Wider gray intervals represent stable fixed absorption crossings from 400 through 4000 `cm^-1`. The published Seiler and Hansen predictions are separated by less than 0.3 meV, while the observation-definition intervals are substantially wider. These intervals show sensitivity to observation definition rather than statistical confidence limits.

## Figure 4

Identifiability diagram for paired HgCdTe gap observations. A calibrated magneto-optical estimate contains the latent gap plus residual measurement uncertainty. An absorption-derived edge contains the same latent gap plus method, carrier, vacancy, and measurement terms. Subtracting the same-specimen magneto-optical estimate from the absorption-derived edge removes the common latent term but leaves observation-specific contributions and residual uncertainty. The diagram explicitly states that the decomposition does not define a universal correction.

## Figure 5

Complete paired `2 x 2 x 2` factorial acquisition design shown in two temperature blocks: target 6 K and 300 K. Each block contains eight specimen states formed by low or high composition, low or high carrier proxy, and low or high vacancy proxy. Every specimen receives paired calibrated magneto-optical and absorption-derived measurements. The design has 16 observations per block, model rank 5 of 5, 11 residual degrees of freedom, condition number 2.618, and maximum leverage 0.4375, for 32 total gap observations across both temperatures. The design is audit-grade only within the declared two-level candidate family and is not a universal optimal-design proof.
