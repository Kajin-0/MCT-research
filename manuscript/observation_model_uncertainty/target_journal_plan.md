# Target journal plan

**Decision date:** 2026-07-19  
**Primary target:** *Infrared Physics & Technology*  
**Article type:** Research Paper  
**Fallback:** *Journal of Electronic Materials*  
**Stretch target:** *Journal of Applied Physics*

## Decision

Submit first to *Infrared Physics & Technology* (IRPT).

The manuscript is centered on infrared material characterization, HgCdTe absorption spectroscopy, semiconductor physics, validated observation modeling, and detector-relevant interpretation. IRPT's current scope explicitly includes infrared materials and devices, material properties and characterization, spectroscopy, solid-state physics, detectors, and appropriately validated advances in modeling techniques. The paper contains real experimental spectra and is not purely synthetic or computation-only.

The principal contribution is also directly addressed to infrared scientists and detector engineers: a few-meV material-law comparison can be reversed by the absorption-edge observation definition, and a paired acquisition design is required to identify the latent gap. This audience fit is sharper than the broader electronic-materials remit of *Journal of Electronic Materials* and more defensible than claiming the broad applied-physics significance expected by *Journal of Applied Physics*.

## Current IRPT submission requirements

The official guide for authors currently requires or recommends:

- editable Word or LaTeX source files;
- numbered sections and subsections;
- a concise factual abstract no longer than 250 words;
- 1-7 English keywords;
- article highlights at submission;
- one designated corresponding author with complete contact information;
- figure captions, table titles, and any supplementary files;
- one to five suggested reviewers, preferably senior, independent, and geographically diverse;
- a CRediT author-contribution statement;
- funding and competing-interest declarations;
- a declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies when such tools were used in manuscript preparation;
- data availability and links to the underlying research data where applicable.

The journal describes the usual Research Paper length as approximately 6-14 printed pages, while stating that content rather than an arbitrary page limit controls length.

## Publishing route

IRPT is a hybrid journal.

- **Subscription route:** no publication fee charged to authors.
- **Optional open access:** the journal currently lists an article publishing charge of USD 2,850 before taxes and possible reductions.

Default plan: use the subscription route unless funding or an institutional agreement covers open access.

### Editorial positioning

The cover letter and introduction should emphasize:

1. real HgCdTe spectra, not a synthetic-only exercise;
2. a validated modeling method that changes an actual material-law conclusion;
3. detector- and spectroscopy-relevant consequences of unspecified absorption-edge definitions;
4. a concrete paired acquisition design rather than another empirical coefficient set;
5. explicit fail-closed claim boundaries and reproducible assets.

Avoid positioning the paper as a universal HgCdTe band-gap equation or as a broad first-principles electronic-structure advance.

## Proposed highlights

- Observation-model choice shifts fitted HgCdTe absorption edges by 6.4-6.8 meV.
- Declared digitization uncertainty moves the fitted edges by no more than 0.9 meV.
- Fixed absorption definitions reverse the nominal closest material-gap relation.
- A sub-meV Seiler-Hansen ordering is not identified without composition uncertainty.
- A paired 2 x 2 x 2 design separates latent-gap, carrier, vacancy, and method terms.

## Fallback and stretch rationale

### Journal of Electronic Materials

Use as the fallback if IRPT considers the contribution too methodological. JEM directly covers semiconductor, photonic, and electronic materials and has an established HgCdTe readership. The disadvantage is that the paper's strongest contribution concerns infrared measurement definition and identifiability rather than synthesis, processing, or a new material property.

### Journal of Applied Physics

Treat as a stretch target only. The manuscript is rigorous, but the present experimental application consists of two digitized spectra from one study. A general applied-physics journal may require broader independent evidence or a stronger universal physical result. Do not broaden claims merely to target JAP.

## Source-control rule

Journal instructions are temporally unstable. Recheck the official IRPT guide immediately before submission, especially the abstract limit, highlights, declaration language, reviewer requirements, file formats, and open-access charge.
