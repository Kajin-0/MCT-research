# Strict manuscript revision summary

## Title

**Extraction-model and fit-domain dependence of reported HgCdTe optical band edges: a two-spectrum case study**

## Scope

The revision analyzes two 300 K absorption-coefficient curves from Moazzami et al. Figure 6. The source curves are outputs of spectroscopic-ellipsometry optical inversion, and the present coordinates were reconstructed from embedded bitmaps. The revision therefore quantifies downstream extraction dependence only; it does not estimate the total experimental uncertainty of either specimen.

## Estimand separation

The primary class contains fitted gap-intercept models:

- free fractional exponent;
- fixed exponents 0.5, 0.7, source-panel exponent, and 1.0;
- provenance-bounded Chu Kane-region form.

Fixed-alpha crossings are separately defined operational coordinates. They are excluded from the fitted-model span and from empirical-equation ranking.

## Model compatibility

Residuals are evaluated in log absorption coordinates and normalized by the local reconstructed source-line half-width. A secondary deterministic screen requires:

- no boundary hit;
- normalized RMS no greater than 1;
- at least 95% of fitted coordinates within two source-line half-widths.

This screen is descriptive only. It is not a likelihood, confidence level, probability, or physical model-selection rule. The complete declared model set remains reported.

## Main conclusion

The revision does not claim that a universal 5-7 meV uncertainty applies to HgCdTe. For these two reconstructed, model-derived spectra, the fitted intercept is materially conditioned by model form, fit-domain endpoints, and point weighting. Fit-domain convention is more influential than the isolated local plateau or the tested reconstruction variants.

## Explicit nonclaims

The revision does not identify:

- a unique physical band gap;
- a total experimental uncertainty budget;
- a physical mechanism for the local plateau;
- a preferred universal HgCdTe gap equation;
- a universal extraction correction;
- a result generalizable beyond the two curves and declared fitted-model family.
