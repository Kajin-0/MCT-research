# Verified manuscript references

**Purpose:** exact bibliography and role ledger for the working manuscript.  
**Policy:** only records checked against primary publisher metadata, institutional records, or the primary article are promoted here. Formatting may later change to match the target journal, but author, title, venue, volume, pages/article number, year, and DOI must not change without renewed verification.

## Material-gap comparators

### Hansen-Schmit-Casselman relation

G. L. Hansen, J. L. Schmit, and T. N. Casselman, “Energy gap versus alloy composition and temperature in Hg1−xCdxTe,” *Journal of Applied Physics* **53**, 7099–7101 (1982). DOI: `10.1063/1.330018`.

**Manuscript role:** established composition- and temperature-dependent material-gap comparator; zero-temperature baseline used by the provisional constrained Hansen-Pade relation.

**Authority status:** verified bibliographic record. This manuscript does not claim that the Hansen relation is exact or universally preferred.

### Published Seiler relation

D. G. Seiler, J. R. Lowney, C. L. Littler, and M. R. Loloee, “Temperature and composition dependence of the energy gap of Hg1−xCdxTe by two-photon magnetoabsorption techniques,” *Journal of Vacuum Science & Technology A* **8**, 1237–1244 (1990). DOI: `10.1116/1.576952`.

**Manuscript role:** published rational-temperature material-gap comparator and prior-art family containing the provisional constrained Hansen-Pade parameterization.

**Authority status:** verified bibliographic record. Seiler is nominally closest for the present fitted-model edges, but strict material-law ranking is not identified.

### Laurenti relation

J. P. Laurenti, J. Camassel, A. Bouhemadou, B. Toulouse, R. Legros, and A. Lusson, “Temperature dependence of the fundamental absorption edge of mercury cadmium telluride,” *Journal of Applied Physics* **67**, 6454–6460 (1990). DOI: `10.1063/1.345119`.

**Manuscript role:** established temperature-dependent material-gap comparator.

**Authority status:** bibliography verified. The repository relation remains a high-confidence reconstruction because the original point-level data, fitting procedure, validity range, and coefficient covariance have not been fully reconstructed from the primary paper.

## Observation models and experimental spectra

### Chu 1994 Kane-region rule

J. Chu, B. Li, K. Liu, and D. Tang, “Empirical rule of intrinsic absorption spectroscopy in Hg1−xCdxTe,” *Journal of Applied Physics* **75**, 1234–1235 (1994). DOI: `10.1063/1.356464`.

**Manuscript role:** provenance-bound intrinsic-absorption observation candidate in the Kane region.

**Authority status:** formula and bibliographic record verified. The candidate is admitted only for `0.170 <= x <= 0.443` and `77 <= T <= 300 K`; boundary-limited fits are not treated as identified edges.

### Moazzami 2005 real-spectrum application

K. Moazzami, J. Phillips, D. Lee, S. Krishnamurthy, G. Benoit, Y. Fink, and T. Tiwald, “Detailed study of above bandgap optical absorption in HgCdTe,” *Journal of Electronic Materials* **34**, 773–778 (2005). DOI: `10.1007/s11664-005-0019-3`.

**Manuscript role:** primary source for the two calibrated 300 K IRSE spectra analyzed in Figures 1–3 and Tables 1–4.

**Authority status:** primary article verified. The repository uses digitized derived spectra with complete calibration provenance; copyrighted source pages and source figures are not redistributed.

## Primary support for observation-state terms

### Carrier filling and Burstein-Moss shift

G. Sarusi, A. Zemel, D. Eger, and Y. Shapira, “Electrical and optical properties of Cu- and Ga-doped Hg1−xCdxTe layers grown by liquid-phase epitaxy,” *Journal of Applied Physics* **65**, 672–676 (1989). DOI: `10.1063/1.343102`.

**Manuscript role:** HgCdTe-specific primary evidence that high free-electron concentration can produce a strong Burstein-Moss shift of the absorption edge.

**Authority status:** verified primary bibliographic record and mechanism relevance. The present manuscript uses `Delta_carrier` as an identifiability term, not as a fitted universal correction.

### Mercury-vacancy influence on the absorption edge

F. Yue, J. Shao, X. Lü, W. Huang, J. Chu, J. Wu, X. Lin, and L. He, “Anomalous temperature dependence of absorption edge in narrow-gap HgCdTe semiconductors,” *Applied Physics Letters* **89**, 021912 (2006). DOI: `10.1063/1.2221411`.

**Manuscript role:** HgCdTe-specific primary evidence that mercury-vacancy states can alter absorption-edge behavior and compromise a conventional optical gap criterion.

**Authority status:** verified title, author list, venue, article number, year, and DOI. The present manuscript uses `Delta_vacancy` as an identifiability term, not as a fitted universal correction.
