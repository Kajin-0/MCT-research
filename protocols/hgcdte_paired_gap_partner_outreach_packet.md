## 2. Concise project brief

### Working title

Paired magneto-optical and absorption measurements for observation-controlled HgCdTe band-gap inference

### Scientific problem

Published HgCdTe gap values combine different observation classes, composition methods, carrier states, vacancy states, and transition models. Historical composition uncertainty and edge-definition effects are commonly larger than the differences among empirical gap equations.

The proposed experiment measures the same registered regions of the same specimens with absorption and magneto-optical methods at approximately 6 K and 300 K. Independent composition, carrier-state, and vacancy-sensitive measurements are retained for every specimen.

### Staged request

The immediate request is a technical feasibility review, not commitment to the full experiment.

Stage 1 is a two-specimen logistics pilot:

```text
2 specimens x 2 optical modalities x 2 temperatures = 8 primary observations
```

The pilot evaluates specimen handling, registered-area transfer, sample-temperature control, calibration, native-data delivery, repeatability, and state drift. It cannot identify independent carrier or vacancy effects.

The full study is authorized only after a measured candidate pool yields eight specimens satisfying the frozen composition, state-separation, polarity, and aliasing gates.

### Non-negotiable scientific requirements

- same physical specimens and co-registered optical areas;
- independent specimen-level composition with hard maximum `sigma_x <= 0.0015`;
- measured carrier density, polarity, mobility, and uncertainty;
- quantitative vacancy proxy and uncertainty;
- sample temperatures near 6 K and 300 K with measured uncertainty;
- no irreversible processing between paired optical modalities;
- at least two native technical repeats;
- native data, calibration records, processing versions, residuals, and covariance when applicable;
- pre/post state-drift measurement;
- traceable file hashes and compatible data/publication rights.

### Requested first response

A useful first response identifies:

1. whether the facility is willing to discuss this specimen class and measurement;
2. an accountable technical contact;
3. compatible specimen geometry and preparation;
4. the directly measured quantity and any model-conditioned output;
5. realistic temperature, spatial-registration, calibration, and uncertainty capability;
6. native-data and covariance availability;
7. destructive or state-altering steps;
8. access route, approximate scheduling, and cost-status process;
9. the most likely technical blocker.

The complete role-specific question set is in `protocols/hgcdte_paired_gap_candidate_contact_questions.md`.
